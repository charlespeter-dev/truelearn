How can pharmacy program leaders and faculty members determine if students are adequately prepared for the North American Pharmacist Licensure Examination® (NAPLEX®) and will deliver the desired exam performance and outcomes?
Integrating board prep into the curriculum from the start of the pharmacy student’s journey is key. This lets institutions ensure alignment between curricular activities and program goals and allows faculty to track longitudinal performance and surface curricular trends, learning gaps, and remediation needs by cohort and individual students.
In our new whitepaper, we explore this and other solutions that can help schools and faculty overcome challenges in pharmacy education and elevate first-time NAPLEX® pass rates.

  • How learning approaches built on cognitive science and data tracking help break down barriers in pharmacy education
     
  • The powerful effects and benefits of audio-visual mnemonics to accelerate pharmacy students’ learning and boost memory formation and recall
     
  • Why data-powered learning software is key to driving optimal academic performance, test-taking capabilities, and NAPLEX® performance
